# SAP S/4HANA Cloud – End-to-End Business Processes

This repository documents my academic learning of **SAP S/4HANA Cloud** within the MSc Business Information Systems program. The focus of this course was to understand how enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems integrate business processes across different organizational functions to support efficient operations, financial transparency, and digital transformation.

SAP S/4HANA Cloud provides an integrated enterprise platform that enables organizations to manage their operations through **end-to-end business processes**. These processes connect departments such as procurement, production, sales, finance, and project management within a single digital system, allowing organizations to operate more efficiently and make data-driven decisions.


### Introduction to SAP S/4HANA Cloud

The course began with an overview of the SAP S/4HANA Cloud platform and its role in supporting digital enterprises. The system integrates operational processes, analytics, and business data in a single environment, enabling organizations to manage complex operations more efficiently.


### Source-to-Pay

The **Source-to-Pay** process focuses on procurement and supplier management. It covers activities such as supplier selection, purchasing materials, receiving goods, processing supplier invoices, and managing payments. This process demonstrates how supply chain management and financial accounting interact within an ERP system.


### Design-to-Operate

The **Design-to-Operate** process describes how organizations design, produce, and manage products or services. It includes product design, production planning, manufacturing operations, and operational monitoring.


### Lead-to-Cash

The **Lead-to-Cash** process represents the complete sales lifecycle. It starts with identifying potential customers and managing sales opportunities, continues with order processing and delivery, and ends with invoicing and payment collection.


### Lead-to-Cash (Service)

The service-oriented Lead-to-Cash process focuses on service delivery rather than physical product sales. It includes service contracts, service execution, billing, and customer support activities.


### Record-to-Report (Financial Accounting)

The **Record-to-Report process in Financial Accounting (FI)** manages financial transactions and reporting activities. It includes recording accounting entries, financial closing procedures, and generating financial statements.


### Record-to-Report (Controlling)

The **Controlling (CO)** component focuses on internal financial management. It supports cost allocation, budgeting, profitability analysis, and internal reporting used for management decision-making.


### Project Management

The **Project Management process** supports planning, executing, and controlling projects within an organization. It includes scheduling, budgeting, resource allocation, and monitoring project performance.
